Siem Reap Province

Details of Siem Reap Province

Etymology & History

The name Siem Reap (សៀមរាប) means "Defeat of Siam," referencing past conflicts with Thailand. Historically, the city gained significance in the 19th century when French explorers documented the ruins of Angkor, leading to restoration efforts and international recognition.

Economy & Tourism

Siem Reap’s economy is heavily reliant on tourism, with over 50% of jobs linked to the industry. By 2012, tourist arrivals had surpassed two million. The city has developed a strong hospitality sector, with luxury hotels, traditional markets, and vibrant nightlife.

Key Attractions

Angkor Wat & Angkor Thom – The most iconic temple sites in Cambodia.

Museums – The Angkor National Museum and War Museum Cambodia showcase the region’s rich history.

Cultural Experiences – Traditional Apsara dance performances and artisan workshops at Artisans Angkor.

Tonlé Sap Lake – Home to floating villages and diverse wildlife.

Gastronomy

Siem Reap is a culinary hub, featuring popular restaurants such as The Sugar Palm, Embassy, and Cuisine Wat Damnak. Local specialties include prahok (fermented fish paste) and Sombai, an infused rice wine inspired by traditional Khmer flavors.
