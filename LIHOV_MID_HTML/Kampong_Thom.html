Kampong Thom province

Details of Kampong Thom Province

Geography and Location

Kampong Thom is Cambodia's second-largest province by area, situated in the central region of the country. It shares borders with Siem Reap to the northwest, Preah Vihear to the north, Stung Treng to the northeast, Kratie to the east, Kampong Cham and Kampong Chhnang to the south, and the Tonle Sap lake to the west. The provincial capital is Stung Saen, located along the Stung Sen River.

Natural Landscapes

Beyond its historical sites, Kampong Thom features diverse natural landscapes, including forests, rivers, and wildlife sanctuaries, offering a glimpse into Cambodia's rich biodiversity.

Historical Significance

The province boasts a rich history, particularly evident in the pre-Angkorian temple complex of Sambor Prei Kuk, which dates back to the Chenla era. This site, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcases the architectural prowess of early Khmer civilization.

Economy

Kampong Thom's economy is predominantly agricultural. The fertile lands support extensive rice farming, rubber plantations, and fishing activities, contributing significantly to the province's livelihood.
